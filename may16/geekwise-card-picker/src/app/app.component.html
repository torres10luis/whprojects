Card Pick-a-matic

You just pick cards and put them back. So fun it doesn't need to be a game. Tell your friends.

Step 1

The "Pick a card!" button should actually do a thing or two. Have it draw a card from a deck! To do this, you will need to instantiate a Deck from the Deck class. Don't do this on every click. Although you might draw cards from multiple decks or combine multiple decks into one, it's probably best to just use one.

The card drawn should show somewhere.

Step 2

Don't just draw and toss. That's reckless and bad for the environment. Let's make a user's hand (ICard[]). Store the cards we draw from the deck and show them using fancy Angular stuff. Now's the time to shine.

Use the <div> with class card-in-hand. Be one with your Angular structural directives.

Step 3

Each card should have a button that, when clicked, sends the card back into the deck from whence it came.

Step 4

Your Deck instance should have a life bar so you don't accidentally kill it. As you remove cards from the deck, present a rectangle (bar) that shrinks and changes color to something dangerousy. Make it look healthy again if cards are returned to the deck.

Step 5

Finished early? Polish.

Disable the "Pick a card!" button when there's no more cards to draw. Style your cards and maybe the space they chill. Make them look good. Add to your GitHub for more job-cred.
